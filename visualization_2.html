Fake News on Facebook

This chart shows that across the political spectrum, average engagement figures are typically higher for fake news than for content that is accurately reported. Taking nine political Facebook pages, average engagement figures (the mean sum of shares, reactions, and comments per post) are consistently highest for content that is mostly false, with the exception of Occupy Democrats, which did see higher engagement for content that was a mixture of true and false, although it saw a drop in engagement for its mostly false content. None of the mainstream pages analyzed here posted any content that was mostly false, although they did see a rise in engagement for posts that contained a mixture of true and false information than those that were mostly true. This graphic uses Buzzfeed data, which rates the accuracy of all content posted by these nine pages over a week in September 2016 into three categories: posts containing information that is mostly true; posts containing information that is a mixture of true and false; and posts containing information that is mostly false.
